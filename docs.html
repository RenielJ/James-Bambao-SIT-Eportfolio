Reflection

During my On-the-Job Training, my core functions primarily involved monitoring the academic performance of DOST-SEI undergraduate scholars. I was responsible for processing their enrollment and ensuring their financial assistance was handled according to policies and government accounting and auditing rules, covering aspects such as monthly stipends, book allowances, and other school fees. I meticulously prepared periodic reports, ensuring the accuracy of the submitted data and updating and maintaining the undergraduate scholars' database at TUP-Taguig. Additionally, I updated and verified LBP requirements for the E-card processing of new scholars and provided assistance by encoding scholarship-related data for new JLSS scholars in their third year.

In addition to these core functions, I was also involved in various university activities and carried out other related tasks as assigned by my immediate superior. My responsibilities included preparing and following up on office communication letters, troubleshooting the date system for monthly allowances in FINDES, and serving as a receptionist or at the Public Assistance Complaint Desk (PACD). Through these diverse tasks, I gained a comprehensive understanding of the administrative and support functions essential to the effective management of the scholarship program.

Furthermore, my role extended to coordinating with different departments and ensuring seamless communication between scholars and the administrative office. This involved addressing scholar inquiries, providing necessary information, and facilitating a supportive environment for their academic pursuits. My involvement in university activities allowed me to develop interpersonal skills and contribute to the community, fostering a collaborative atmosphere within the institution.

I also played a pivotal role in organizing and participating in events aimed at enhancing the educational experience of the scholars. This included workshops, seminars, and other academic-related events where I assisted in logistics, coordination, and execution. By taking part in these events, I not only enhanced my organizational skills but also gained insights into the broader educational landscape and the importance of extracurricular activities in a student's overall development.

My responsibilities further included maintaining and updating records with meticulous attention to detail, ensuring data integrity, and supporting the administrative processes with efficiency. This experience has honed my ability to manage multiple tasks, prioritize effectively, and adhere to deadlines. Additionally, my exposure to government policies and procedures provided me with a deeper understanding of the regulatory framework governing educational scholarships and financial assistance programs.

In summary, my OJT experience encompassed a wide range of administrative and support functions that contributed significantly to my professional growth. By engaging in these activities, I developed a strong foundation in administrative processes, data management, communication, and event coordination. This comprehensive experience has equipped me with the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in a professional environment and has prepared me to contribute effectively to any organization in the future. s
